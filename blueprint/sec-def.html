2.1 Clifford algebras - definition

Throughout this section:

Let \(M\) be a module over a commutative ring \(R\), equipped with a quadratic form \(Q: M \to R\).

Let \(\iota : M \to _{l[R]} T(M)\) be the canonical \(R\)-linear map for the tensor algebra \(T(M)\).

Let \(\iota _a : R \to _{+*} T(M)\) be the canonical map from \(R\) to \(T(M)\), as a ring homomorphism.

Definition 2.1.1 Clifford relation
#

Lean declarations

  • CliffordAlgebra.Rel

\(\forall m \in M, \iota (m)^2 \sim \iota _a(Q(m))\)

We say that \(\iota \) is Clifford if this relation holds.

Definition 2.1.2 Clifford algebra
#

  • Definition 1.1.23
  • Definition 2.1.1

Lean declarations

  • CliffordAlgebra

A Clifford algebra over \(M\), denoted \(\mathop{\mathcal{C}\ell }(M)\), is the quotient of the tensor algebra \(T(M)\) by Clifford relation 2.1.1.

Remark 2.1.3
#

In literatures, \(M\) is often written \(V\), and the quotient is taken by the two-sided ideal \(I_Q\) generated from the set \(\{ v \otimes v - Q(v) \: \vert \: v \in V \} \).

As of writing, Mathlib does not have direct support for two-sided ideals, but it does support the equivalent operation of taking the quotient by a suitable closure of a relation like \(v \otimes v \sim Q(v)\).

Hence the definition above.

Example 2.1.4 Clifford algebra over a vector space
#

Let \(V\) be a vector space \(\mathbb R^n\) over \(\mathbb R\), equipped with a quadratic form \(Q\).

Since \(\mathbb R\) is a commutative ring and \(V\) is a module, definition 2.1.2 of Clifford algebra applies.
